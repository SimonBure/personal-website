In this Master 2 research project, we studied the dynamics of interactions between vegetation and water resources in an arid ecosystem using a stochastic model.
The objective was to better understand the mechanisms leading to the emergence of self-organized spatial structures, such as spotted or banded patterns observed in nature.
These patterns can be used to quantify the pressures exerted on ecosystems and to prevent desertification.
This probabilistic approach captures the influence of environmental fluctuations on the stability and organization of ecosystems under water stress.
This project received the second highest grade in the master's program.
