This thesis develops a rigorous mathematical model of working memory based on discrete-time neural networks. Each neuron is characterized by its membrane potential and a short-term synaptic facilitation mechanism, modeled as a Markov chain.

The study establishes the conditions for irreducibility of the system and characterizes absorbing spaces to ensure persistent neural activity. Using a mean-field approach, we prove the convergence of the finite model to a limit process and compute the associated stationary measure.

The results reveal a precise sufficient condition for the emergence of non-trivial equilibria allowing information to be maintained in memory.
